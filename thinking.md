# Part 3 — System Thinking Responses


## Question A — The Immediate Response

Hi [Guest Name],

I’m very sorry to hear about the hot water issue, especially with guests arriving for breakfast shortly. I understand how frustrating this must be.

I have immediately flagged this as a high-priority issue and notified the on-ground support team to resolve it as quickly as possible. A team member will contact you shortly with an update and assistance regarding the inconvenience caused.

Thank you for your patience.

### Why this response?

The message acknowledges the guest’s frustration immediately while maintaining a calm and professional tone. It avoids making refund promises automatically at 3am while still assuring urgent escalation and human follow-up.

---

## Question B — The System Design

The platform should classify this message as a high-priority complaint and automatically trigger an escalation workflow.

The system should:
- Notify the operations manager immediately via SMS or mail
- Alert the on-ground caretaker
- Create an incident log for the property
- Mark the conversation as urgent in the dashboard

If no human responds within 30 minutes, the system should escalate again to a backup manager and continue sending reminders until acknowledged.

---

## Question C — The Learning

If multiple hot water complaints occur within a short time period, the platform should identify it as a recurring operational issue.

I would build a property health monitoring system that tracks complaint frequency by category. If complaints cross a threshold, the system should automatically:
- Create preventive maintenance tasks
- Increase operational risk score for the property
- Notify internal teams proactively
- Recommend temporary inspection before future bookings

This would help prevent repeated guest dissatisfaction and improve long-term operational reliability.
